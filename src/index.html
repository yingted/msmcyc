About Us

The Multiple Sclerosis Mississauga Youth Committee (MSMYC) is a youth-run organization that advocates for awareness of multiple sclerosis in youth. As a non-profit organization that works closely with the MS Society of Canada, the MSMYC was founded by Cathy Li in January 2012 in an effort to bring the issue of multiple sclerosis to the forefront of youth, while raising money towards finding a cure and enhancing the lives of those affected.

What is Multiple Sclerosis?

Multiple sclerosis is a chronic disease, often progressive, that affects the central nervous system, resulting in loss of muscle control and sensation. While Canada has the highest incident rate of multiple sclerosis in the world, the majority of the youth populace remains unaware of this disease and its impact. To address this, the MSMYC emphasizes awareness, education, and action through the youth populace. We place high value in the educational background of the disease, as it is an important step in understanding how to advocate for those facing MS as well as in sparking interest in the disease.

Mission Statement

The Multiple Sclerosis Mississauga Youth Committee (MSMYC) strives to engage, educate, and empower youth to take active measures and become leaders in the fight against multiple sclerosis by stimulating awareness, instilling a sense of understanding and empathy for those affected, and raising money towards finding a cure.

Vision

My first encounter with multiple sclerosis was at a volunteering event in grade nine. As a curious, science-loving student, I did not hesitate to investigate further into this disease and since then, I’ve worked with the MS Society of Canada as the Volunteer Coordinator of the MS Walk in Streetsville for the last three years. Having worked with both adult and youth volunteers, I realized that although the MS Society is successful in reaching out to adults, it was less successful in engaging the younger demographic. This was the fuelling agent to the start of the MS Youth Committee. I initiated this committee in hopes of inspiring others to take active measures in understanding this complex disease and become leaders in the fight against multiple sclerosis. I know that when given the opportunity, youth have the potential to make a significant impact in the world, especially in an area of concern, such as multiple sclerosis, that can affect anyone anytime. Already we have established a large network of student ambassadors in high schools across Mississauga, working together to promote awareness and understanding of multiple sclerosis through youth-oriented fundraisers and events. We’ve taken the first step, and I truly hope that youth, hand-in-hand, will join us in the movement for an MS-free future together.

— Cathy Li
Founder and Chair
MS Mississauga Youth Committee
