Vision

My first encounter with multiple sclerosis was at a volunteering event in grade nine. As a curious, science-loving student, I did not hesitate to investigate further into this disease and since then, I’ve worked with the MS Society of Canada as the Volunteer Coordinator of the MS Walk in Streetsville for the last three years. Having worked with both adult and youth volunteers, I realized that although the MS Society is successful in reaching out to adults, it was less successful in engaging the younger demographic. This was the fuelling agent to the start of the MS Youth Committee. I initiated this committee in hopes of inspiring others to take active measures in understanding this complex disease and become leaders in the fight against multiple sclerosis. I know that when given the opportunity, youth have the potential to make a significant impact in the world, especially in an area of concern, such as multiple sclerosis, that can affect anyone anytime. Already we have established a large network of student ambassadors in high schools across Mississauga, working together to promote awareness and understanding of multiple sclerosis through youth-oriented fundraisers and events. We’ve taken the first step, and I truly hope that youth, hand-in-hand, will join us in the movement for an MS-free future together.

— Cathy Li
Founder and Chair
MS Mississauga Youth Committee

ChairCathy Li

Cathy is currently completing her senior year in the International Baccalaureate Program. Aside from her involvement with the MS Society of Canada, she actively volunteers at her local hospital and is a Shad Valley Queen’s 2012 alumnus. As an avid musician and athlete, she teaches piano, and loves to be actively competing in sports, such as volleyball and soccer. Head of Corporate AffairsNeil Liu

Neil Liu is a vocal Grade 10 member in the community and attending the IB Program. As an active figure in the community, he has participated in many social events, as well as volunteered. He thinks the most important thing about MSMYC is the cooperation of students from different schools to accomplish a task and he really hopes that MSMYC can make a difference. In his opinion, youth can greatly benefit from participating in a community effort to support the excellent work of researchers and use this opportunity to get closer with their communities, take an active role in an organization, exercise their leadership capabilities, and acquire the skills they will need to apply throughout their lives. Raising money for a significant cause like the search for a cure for multiple sclerosis is, in his view, the perfect opportunity for aspiring leaders to shine.

Head of Student AmbassadorsAllison Chen

Allison is a driven peer and leader who strives to consistently and actively participate within her school and community through various extracurriculars and volunteer engagements, which span across Interact, cross country,show choir, to volunteering at the hospital. Through her involvement with MSMYC, she aims to encourage other youth to get involved and further develop the growth of mere interests into passions. Any spare time she has is dedicated, for the most part, to bettering herself and the community, with a vision to create an awareness of youth potential and engagement within the community to create long lasting positive change on our generation and the generations to follow.
